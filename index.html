🏠The Story

We spent two and a half years looking for a vacation home in the mountains near a lake—somewhere for us to rest, and somewhere to build memories with family and friends. Growing up, my extended cousins had places on the Chesapeake Bay and Shore, and I wanted the same thing for my own family.

One winter between Christmas and New Year, we rented a home on the west side of Seneca Lake near Dundee. Even in the cold, the place was stunning. Hidden gems everywhere—small towns, good restaurants, shops, trails, waterfalls, views. We fell in love immediately.

Our search had been slowing down. We weren't finding what we wanted. But when we came back to look at a property with our realtor, I spotted this house three miles away. We asked if we could see it. She got us in that same morning.

The house wasn't in great shape. The land was overgrown. But we saw what it could be. Our families were worried we'd taken on too much. The closing was hard—delayed from July to October 2023—but we finally closed in October.

Before we owned it, this was a bed and breakfast in the nineties and early two thousands. The Reading House. That history matters to us.

It's a large federal-style home with five bedrooms, five baths, original trim and details worth preserving. Our renovation has been about honoring those bones while bringing it back to life—sanding, painting, staining, replacing what needed it, making things work in a house that doesn't sit perfectly square.

The stairwell is our statement piece. The railings are so ornate. We gave it the attention it deserves.

We can't be here full time because of our careers. But we can share it. We want you to make your own memories here, with the people you love, and discover everything the Finger Lakes has to offer.

ℹ️Things to Know About the House

Check-in & House Rules
Check-in and check-out times are listed on the platform you booked through (Airbnb, VRBO, or fingerlakescottages.com).

Quiet Hours: 10pm to 7am. We ask that you keep gatherings to a reasonable size—this is a home in a residential area. No smoking indoors.

Dogs: Dogs are welcome if identified when you book. We have a fenced yard, one dog bed, and dog bowls for food and water. Couch covers are available in the wicker basket in the main living room if you'd like to use them. Please pick up after your dog—there's a small trash can in the fenced yard for waste. Dog waste bags and trash bags for the dog waste can are located near the washer in the laundry room. Put the waste in the large outdoor trash can before you leave.

Water & Well System
The house runs on well water and septic. Our water is treated and potable straight from the tap. You may notice some air in the water system, especially when hot water is turned on—this is normal and harmless.

Filtered water for drinking and coffee is available at the bar sink in the kitchen (reverse osmosis).

Hot water: We have one water heater. If multiple people shower at the same time, hot water may run out. The system reheats in about 20-30 minutes. We ask for patience—it's part of the charm of an older home.

Climate Control
Each bedroom has its own window AC unit and ceiling fan. The green front living rooms share one AC unit, and the dining room in the back has its own. Turn off AC units when you're done with your stay.

If renting in winter, we have a propane furnace with space heaters available. Propane is expensive, so we heat efficiently.

Kitchen & Coffee
A full 12-cup coffee pot and single-cup Keurig are provided. Coffee k-cups are in the cabinet under the coffee sink. Filtered water for coffee is at the bar sink.

Other appliances available: electric tea kettle, 4-slice toaster, crock pot, blender, electric griddle, microwave, dishwasher, food processor, mixing bowls, various pots and pans (including cast iron), baking sheets, cutting boards, and more.

A gas grill is provided with two propane tanks. If the first tank runs low, you can exchange it in Watkins Glen (Walmart, Watkins Supply, 7-11) or Dundee (Dandy Mart, Dollar General).

Linens & Laundry
The hallway bathroom on the second floor has a linen closet stocked with extra bath towels, hand towels, and sheets if you need them. Hair dryers are in the primary bedroom and hallway bathroom. Washer and dryer are available for your use.

Windows & Doors
Most windows are original sash windows from the 1820s. The pulleys have been cut, so windows require props to stay open—these are provided in each frame. On nice days, we recommend opening the front entrance, side entrance, and back doors to let fresh air through. Storm windows are also available.

Outdoor Space & Fire Pit
The property has a fire pit. We do not provide firewood. Firewood is available for purchase on the side road up and down NY-14. Our one direct neighbor also has it readily available. Walmart, gas stations, Watkins Supply, and the U-Haul down the street may also have it to purchase. For any side road pick ups, make sure to have cash.

Fire pit rules: Use dry, appropriate firewood. Keep the fire attended at all times—never leave it unattended. Fully extinguish before bed or before you leave. Keep the fire at a safe distance from structures and trees. No glass bottles around the fire pit.

Yard space is available across the property except beyond the pond area, which is our neighbors' land. The property is bordered by the north side with Rock Stream (covered with trees) and the south side with a small stream that feeds into our two ponds. Please be kind and respectful of our neighbors on the south side. On rainy days, areas outside the fenced section may be muddy.

Parking
Two gravel driveways can accommodate up to 6 cars. Park on either one.

Trash & Recycling
Trash and recycling are picked up on Thursday. Please put them out before you leave. Trash cans are located behind the garage, next to the deck. All garbage must be bagged. Recyclables must be bagged and placed in a separate trash can from garbage. Do not place items on top of or in garbage cans—anything on the can will be assumed to be trash.

Entertainment
A Smart TV is in the living room. The primary bedroom has a Samsung TV with a Roku streaming stick—use the Roku for streaming services, not the Samsung's built-in apps. Sign in with your own streaming accounts. Roku Live TV is free and offers good viewing options.

Board games, jigsaw puzzles, and cards are available in the front green living rooms. The middle living room has a soft-tip dart board.

WiFi: The network name and password are available on your booking site. You'll also find a QR code in the home that you can scan to connect automatically. We've remotely worked from the home for multiple days and weeks at a time—the connection is reliable for working.

A Word About the House
This home was built in 1820. The floors aren't all level—please have caution on stairs and uneven areas. It's an old house with character, original wood siding, and all the quirks that come with age. Bugs are common in a farm area (ladybugs, boxelder beetles, stink bugs). We take preventative measures, but you may see insects typical to the region. It's part of living near nature.

We hope you love it as much as we do.

🥾Trails

A short list of hikes nearby, ranging from easy gorge walks to longer loops in the national forest.

  • Watkins Glen State Park — Gorge Trail ~10 min south · ~2 mi · moderate (many stairs) Iconic stone path past 19 waterfalls and 200-ft cliffs. The gorge trail is closed in winter; rim trails stay open. Park info → Directions →
  • Seneca Mills Trail & Cascade Falls ~8 min northeast · easy Short walk to a waterfall and historic mill site. Beautiful and peaceful. Directions →
  • Havana Glen Park — Eagle Cliff Falls ~15 min south (Montour Falls) · short walk · easy A small county park with a quick walk in to a 41-ft waterfall — good if you want a waterfall without the Watkins Glen crowds. Park info → Directions →
  • Finger Lakes National Forest — Interloken / Burnt Hill ~25–30 min east (Hector) · 3–12 mi options · easy to moderate The only national forest in NY. Mix of woods, meadows, and pasture; the Interloken Trail runs ~12 mi north–south, with shorter loops like Burnt Hill (~3.8 mi). USDA Forest Service → Directions →
  • Catharine Valley Trail multiple access points · flat · easy Year-round rail trail through Schuyler County for walking, biking, snowshoeing. Free. Trail info → Directions →
  • Queen Catharine Marsh Loop ~10 min south of Watkins Glen · ~7 mi · level Quiet wildlife-management-area trail through marsh and woods — good for birding. DEC info → Directions →
  • Seneca Lake State Park — Lakefront Trail ~45 min north (Geneva) · ~4.3 mi · easy/paved Flat lakefront walk. Pair with a stroll through downtown Geneva. Park info → Directions →

  • Geneva ~45 min north · north end of Seneca Lake Walkable historic downtown on the lakefront, 19th-century architecture, lakefront state park, and the start of the Seneca Lake Wine Trail's east side. Visitor info → Directions →
  • Penn Yan ~40 min west · Keuka Lake Compact downtown with shops, lakefront access, and a different (Y-shaped) Finger Lake to explore. Easy half-day. Visitor info → Directions →
